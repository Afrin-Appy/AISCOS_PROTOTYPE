Key Performance Indicators

Evaluation metrics used to measure AI effectiveness across each research priority

RQ1 · Production Forecasting
Forecasting Accuracy KPIs
RMSE — Root Mean Squared Error
Penalises large forecast errors heavily. Primary metric — lower RMSE means fewer costly over/under-production events.
MAE — Mean Absolute Error
Average error in original production units. Directly maps to surplus stock or shortage volume per period.
MAPE — Mean Absolute % Error
Scale-free percentage error. Enables fair model comparison across different product categories and SKU sizes.
R² — Coefficient of Determination
Variance explained by the model. Closer to 1.0 means the model captures underlying demand patterns well.
RQ2 · Defect Detection
Classification Quality KPIs
Accuracy — Overall Correct Rate
Proportion of all products correctly labelled. Primary metric for overall classifier reliability.
Precision — Positive Predictive Value
Of all flagged defects, how many were truly defective. High precision avoids scrapping good products unnecessarily.
Recall — Sensitivity / Detection Rate
Of all real defects, how many were caught. High recall prevents faulty products from reaching end customers.
F1-Score — Harmonic Mean of P & R
Balances Precision and Recall into one score. Key metric when defect datasets are imbalanced.
RQ3 · Inventory Optimization
Inventory Efficiency KPIs
MAE — Mean Absolute Error
Average inventory forecast error in units. Ties directly to holding cost volume and stockout frequency.
RMSE — Root Mean Squared Error
Amplifies large misses. Critical for preventing sudden stockout events or extreme overstock situations.
MAPE — Mean Absolute % Error
Percentage-based error for comparing inventory accuracy across SKUs of varying demand volumes.
WAPE — Weighted Absolute % Error
Volume-weighted error metric. Accounts for demand scale per SKU — best for aggregate inventory decisions.

RQ1 – Production Forecasting

Time-series forecasting models evaluated on FreshRetailNet-50K (4.85M rows, fresh retail)

↗ HuggingFace: FreshRetailNet-50K
★ Best Model: SARIMAX — Lowest RMSE (207.56) and MAE (159.10) on FreshRetailNet-50K. SARIMAX captures both seasonal patterns and exogenous variables, outperforming all other models on the primary accuracy KPIs.

RQ1 – Production Forecasting (T1 / PVC Manufacturing Dataset)

Time-series forecasting on the own T1 dataset — local PVC manufacturing supply chain data

↗ Google Sheets: T1 Dataset
★ Best Valid Model: ARIMA — Lowest reliable RMSE (410.67), MAE (410.66), and MAPE (5.12%) on the T1 PVC dataset. Although SARIMA and SARIMAX show lower raw numbers (RMSE/MAE: 385.13, MAPE: 3.16%), those results are not meaningful — the T1 dataset has no seasonal patterns, so seasonal models overfit to noise rather than real signal, producing misleading metrics. ARIMA's non-seasonal structure is the correct and trustworthy model for this data.

RQ2 – Manufacturing Defect Detection

Classification models on two datasets: Kaggle Manufacturing Defects & DataCo Supply Chain

★ Best Model: Random Forest — Highest Accuracy (95.06%), Recall (99.27%) and F1-Score (97.13%) on Kaggle Full Features. Also best on DataCo with 81.15% Accuracy. Random Forest consistently outperforms all other classifiers across both datasets.

RQ3 – Inventory Optimization

Demand forecasting models evaluated for inventory management on FreshRetailNet-50K

↗ HuggingFace: FreshRetailNet-50K
★ Best Overall Model: SARIMA — Achieves the lowest MAE (1,344.27) and lowest RMSE (1,995.96) making it the best model on absolute inventory unit errors. These KPIs directly determine stockout volume and holding cost, making SARIMA the primary recommendation for inventory optimization.

Note: ARIMAX has slightly lower WAPE (6.68%) and MAPE (6.75%) on percentage metrics. However SARIMA dominates on absolute error (MAE & RMSE) which matters most for actual inventory decision-making.

⚙️ How This Works — Full Technical Explanation

What you enter & why

1
General context (category, region, SKUs, month) gives the AI the business environment so it can adjust predictions for seasonality, market behaviour, and scale.
2
Demand fields (current sales, 3-month average, trend %, seasonal factor) are the inputs the SARIMAX-style forecasting logic uses to project next month's required production volume.
3
Defect fields (planned units, current defect rate, machine age, maintenance status) feed the Random Forest-inspired classifier that estimates how many units will be defective in the next production run.
4
Inventory fields (stock on hand, lead time, holding cost, stockout penalty) are used by the SARIMA-style optimizer to calculate the recommended safety stock level that minimizes total cost.

Backend & AI engine

5
Pre-computation: Before calling the AI, the system runs formula-based estimates locally in your browser using the exact parameter weights derived from our research models (SARIMAX RMSE 207.56, RF Acc 95.06%, SARIMA MAE 1344.27).
6
AI API call: Your inputs plus the pre-computed estimates are sent to the Anthropic Claude API (claude-sonnet-4) via a secure POST request. Claude acts as an expert supply chain analyst that interprets your data with full domain reasoning.
7
Structured output: Claude is instructed to respond only in JSON format with exact integer predictions and brief explanations for each of the three research questions — demand, defects, and safety stock.
8
Fallback safety net: If the API is unavailable, the system automatically falls back to the local formula estimates so you always get results — a yellow notice will appear indicating this.

Models powering the predictions

SARIMAX → RQ1 Demand Random Forest → RQ2 Defects SARIMA → RQ3 Inventory
A
SARIMAX (best on FreshRetailNet-50K, RMSE 207.56): A Seasonal ARIMA model with eXogenous variables. It models seasonal demand cycles plus external factors like trend and promotions to forecast next-month production demand.
B
Random Forest (Accuracy 95.06%, Recall 99.27%): An ensemble of decision trees that classifies products as defective or not. Machine age, maintenance history, and defect rate are key features. High recall ensures nearly all real defects are caught.
C
SARIMA (best MAE 1,344.27 on FreshRetailNet-50K): Seasonal ARIMA applied to inventory demand. Its safety stock recommendation uses: daily demand × lead time × safety factor, adjusted for demand variability between current and historical sales.

How to interpret the results

i
Predicted Production Demand — the number of units your supply chain should plan to produce next month. Use this as your production target, adjusted for lead time and current stock.
ii
Predicted Defective Units — the estimated number of units that will fail quality checks in the next production run. Subtract this from planned output to get your expected saleable yield.
iii
Recommended Safety Stock — the buffer inventory level that protects against demand spikes and supply delays. If your current stock is below this number, you should reorder immediately.
iv
AI Analysis section — the written recommendations from Claude are derived from integrating all three results. They identify the most urgent action, highlight risks, and suggest the optimal next step across your supply chain.
